SafeSpace Initiative

Baby theft involves unlawfully taking an infant from their rightful parents or guardians. It is a distressing and prevalent crime worldwide. Yet, comprehensive statistical records on its prevalence in Ghana are lacking. Secondary data analysis reveals that poverty, socio-economic inequality, cultural beliefs surrounding childlessness, and the demand for babies for adoption or labor are significant drivers of this crime. Perpetrators include individuals or gangs involved in abduction and trafficking, corrupt adoption agencies, medical personnel, and complicit family members. High-profile cases, such as the Ridge Hospital incident in December 2023 (Suspected ‘Baby Thief’ Busted At Ridge Hospital - DailyGuide Network , 2023), highlight the severity of the issue. Strategies for prevention include community awareness, regulatory measures, law enforcement, and international cooperation. This project sought to develop an IoT based baby security crib for hospitals which incorporates a two-step security verification system, finger print sensors for the crib and an RFID identification system for the door. The authentication system was to take records of people who enter the hospital nursery and alert the hospital of any unauthorized access to both the baby ward and the baby's crib.
